Project Overview

The Automated Curtain Opener is a smart device designed to lift and lower a window curtain using a motorized system. It combines mechanical design, 3D-printed components, and electronics to create a hands-free solution for curtain control. The project demonstrates skills in prototyping, digital fabrication, and system integration.

Key Features

  • Motorized curtain movement for effortless opening and closing
  • Custom 3D-printed gear system to increase torque and ensure smooth operation
  • Limit switches to prevent over-rotation and protect the motor
  • Programmable schedule for daily operations
  • Manual override capability

Technical Implementation

The device is powered by a DC motor connected to a custom-designed, 3D-printed gear system. The system was programmed on an Arduino microcontroller using C++ to control curtain movement through button inputs. A pulley and cord mechanism was used to attach the curtain to the gear system, allowing it to be raised or lowered. To ensure precise stopping points, limit switches were integrated into the design, automatically cutting power when the curtain reached its open or closed position.

Technologies Used:

  • Hardware: Arduino microcontroller, DC motor, motor driver, limit switches, custom 3D-Printed components
  • Software: Arduino IDE (C++), basic control algorithms
  • Design: Fusion360 CAD, Tinkercad
  • Fabrication: Breadboarding, soldering, mechanical rigging for curtain actuation

Challenges & Solutions

  • Torque limitations: The motor initially lacked power to lift the curtain. This was solved by designing and 3D-printing custom gears to increase torque.
  • Over-rotation:The curtain would overshoot the desired end points. Limit switches were added to stop motion at the correct positions.
  • Inconsistent motion: The curtain sometimes slipped or jerked. Cord alignment and tension were refined to ensure smooth and reliable movement

Results

The final prototype successfully lifts and lowers the curtain on command or on preset time, demonstrating smooth, controlled, and repeatable operation. The integration of custom 3D-printed gears and limit switches proved critical to performance, and the project showcased the practical combination of mechanical design, electronics, and fabrication.
