Project Overview

Designed and simulated a functional printed circuit board (PCB) as part of a bootcamp project, demonstrating practical skills in circuit design, layout optimization, and digital fabrication preparation. The project highlighted proficiency in both electronics theory and applied PCB design using professional software tools.

Key Features

  • Fully designed schematics with correct component placement and signal routing
  • Optimized PCB layout to minimize noise, crosstalk, and signal interference
  • Prepared board for fabrication-ready output, including Gerber files
  • Simulated circuits to verify functionality before physical production

Technical Implementation

The PCB was created using Altium Designer, where schematics were drawn and components were selected and placed strategically to optimize performance. Signal traces were routed to minimize electrical noise and interference, and the board was simulated to verify proper functionality. Design rules and constraints were applied to ensure the PCB would be fabrication-ready, and Gerber files were generated for potential production. Iterative testing of the schematic and layout allowed for error detection and correction before finalizing the board.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Complex signal routing: The motor initially lacked power to lift the curtain. This was solved by designing and 3D-printing custom gears to increase torque.Managing multiple connections in a compact layout was difficult. This was solved by careful layer planning and trace optimization.
  • Component placement constraints:Limited board space required strategic placement; iterative layout adjustments ensured proper fit and function.
  • Simulation accuracy: Ensuring simulated behavior matched expected performance required repeated verification and tweaking of circuit parameters.

Results

The final PCB design was fully simulation-verified and ready for fabrication, demonstrating strong proficiency in circuit design, PCB layout, and electronic system integration. The project showcased the ability to apply theoretical knowledge in a practical, industry-standard design environment.
